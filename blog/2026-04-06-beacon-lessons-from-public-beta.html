Beacon taught me to respect the messy middle

By Bryce Murad · April 06, 2026

What public beta actually means

Public does not mean proven

Beacon being public does not mean every business claim around it is proven. That distinction matters. A route can be live. A workflow can be useful. A product can still be pre-revenue or not yet proven at scale.

The messy middle is where most products actually live. You have enough built to learn from reality, but not enough evidence to brag like the scoreboard is settled.

The useful lesson

Beacon forced the right questions. How do you find businesses without turning outreach into spam? How do you generate a useful demo without inventing facts? How do you track attribution? How do you make local-service marketing useful instead of annoying?

Those questions are more valuable than a fake victory lap.

What comes next

The path forward is receipts: real operators, real outreach, real responses, clean compliance, and product loops that make sense for the people receiving them. That is slower than making a loud claim. It is also how you build something durable.
