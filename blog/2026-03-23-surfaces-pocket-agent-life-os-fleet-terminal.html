Surfaces are where the agent stack meets real life

By Bryce Murad · March 23, 2026

Pocket Agent, Life OS, and Fleet Terminal

Interfaces decide whether infrastructure matters

A great agent stack that nobody can use is just a science fair project with better logging. Surfaces are the answer to that problem. They are the human layer on top of Cortex.

Pocket Agent is the voice and intent surface. Personal Life OS is the calendar, todo, and daily operating layer. Fleet Terminal is the interface for seeing and steering the machines. The point is not to add more apps for the sake of it. The point is to make the stack reachable in the moments when I actually need it.

Voice is messy, which is why it matters

Most real intent starts messy. You say something half-formed while walking, coaching, driving, or trying to get out the door. Pocket Agent should catch that, clean it up, and turn it into something agents can parallelize through Agent Fabric.

That is what I mean by punched-up intent. Not making the request fancy. Making it executable.

The daily layer

Personal Life OS matters because work does not happen in a vacuum. Calendar, tasks, reminders, priorities, context, and energy all affect what actually gets done. Fleet Terminal matters because the machines are part of the workflow. Even Realities points at a future where the interface is not always a laptop.

Surfaces are how the stack gets out of the basement and into the day.
