Beacon is a practical lead engine, not magic

By Bryce Murad · February 16, 2026

What the architecture is supposed to do

Start with a real business problem

Beacon exists because a lot of local service businesses are still hard to find, hard to evaluate, or poorly represented online. The product idea is straightforward: find real businesses, understand their public presence, and create a better path from research to outreach.

I am careful with how I talk about Beacon now. It is not some money printer. It is not proof that AI can replace sales. It is a working direction for local-service lead generation, demo-site workflows, and operator-controlled outreach.

The useful part is the pipeline

The architecture is only interesting if it reduces friction. Discovery finds candidate businesses. Enrichment gives the agent useful context. The demo and outreach layers turn that context into something a human can review. The CRM layer keeps the work from turning into a pile of tabs.

The agent should help with research and first drafts. It should not invent facts, spam people, or pretend every lead is a customer. That line matters.

What I still have to prove

The next proof is not a louder landing page. It is receipts: real conversations, clean attribution, compliant outreach, and a product loop that makes sense for the businesses it serves. Until then, Beacon is an active build with a public route and a lot of useful infrastructure behind it.
