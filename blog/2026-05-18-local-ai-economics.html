Local AI economics are not obvious

By Bryce Murad · May 18, 2026

Where the money actually goes

Cloud costs hide in convenience

Cloud AI is convenient until the usage pattern gets weird. Agents can burn tokens because they are curious, stuck, or poorly scoped. A few bad loops can turn into a bill that teaches you religion.

Local models change the equation, but they do not make everything free. Hardware costs money. Power costs money. Maintenance costs time. The advantage is control and repeatability, not magic savings.

Route with intent

The best cost strategy is not local-only or cloud-only. It is routing. Use cheap local models where they are good enough. Use frontier models when the task deserves it. Cache what can be cached. Stop sending low-stakes work to expensive models because it was easy to wire.

That is why Maestro matters. Cost control is an architecture problem.

Measure before bragging

I am deliberately careful with cost claims now. If I cannot point to the measurement, I should not turn it into a headline. The archive cleanup follows the same rule. No fake five-figure AWS bills. No pretend years of operating history. The numbers have to survive daylight.
