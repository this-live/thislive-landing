SAQ reminds me software can be service

By Bryce Murad · May 11, 2026

Community tools without the startup cosplay

Not every project is a company

SAQ and Storrs Aquatics sit in a different category for me. They are community and coaching support, not a commercial This.Live product. That matters. The goal is not to turn every useful thing into a startup.

Sometimes software is just service. Make the website steadier. Make meet information easier to find. Reduce handoff pain. Help parents, swimmers, and coaches spend less time fighting systems.

Coaching changes the product lens

When you coach, you see how much friction families absorb. Schedules, emails, meets, warmups, entries, fees, directions, last-minute changes. A small tool that removes confusion can matter more than a flashy product demo.

That is a useful reminder for the rest of This.Live too. Good software respects the people using it.

The right boundary

The boundary is important: support the community, do not overclaim ownership, and do not disrupt systems that already work. Zero-disruption handoffs beat heroic rewrites almost every time.
