Fieldhouse and FTAG are two different game lanes

By Bryce Murad · April 13, 2026

Sports games and Future Throwback Arcade Games

The distinction matters

Fieldhouse and FTAG should not blur together. Fieldhouse is the sports-game lane: underserved sports, arcade feel, mobile-first loops, and a path toward a broader portfolio. FTAG is Future Throwback Arcade Games: original spiritual successors to the games that made browser-era and early mobile games feel immediate.

That difference matters because the design instincts are different. A sports game has real-world constraints. A throwback arcade game has memory, pacing, and feel as its source material.

Ages and Arrows

The first FTAG lane is Ages and Arrows. The inspiration is not to copy old games. It is to capture the part that worked: readable pressure, upgrades that change how you play, simple inputs, and enough mastery to make another run feel worth it.

The Balatro lesson is not jokers. It is the way between-run upgrades make players feel their power curve changing. That is the part worth studying.

Build with taste

Games punish vague thinking quickly. If the loop is boring, the player leaves. If the numbers feel fake, the player feels it. That is why this lane needs prototypes, playtests, and honest iteration more than branding.
