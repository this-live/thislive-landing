The fleet is part of the product

By Bryce Murad · March 09, 2026

Why the machines matter

Local AI is physical

Cloud dashboards make infrastructure feel weightless. Local AI does not. It has machines, fans, disks, GPUs, network paths, SSH sessions, and the occasional mystery failure that makes you question your life choices at midnight.

My fleet matters because This.Live is not only a set of web pages. It is a working environment for agents. Jarvis, Fred, Atlas, Sparky, Bob, and NAS each play a role. Some are for coordination. Some are for storage. Some are for model work. Some are for experiments.

The network is the company nervous system

Tailscale makes the mesh manageable. SSH makes it honest. If I cannot get to a machine, inspect logs, move files, and prove what is running, I do not really operate it.

That comes straight from my network background. Diagrams are nice. Reachability is better. A system is only real when you can troubleshoot it under pressure.

Why I keep it local

Local infrastructure is not always cheaper. It is not always easier. But it gives me control, privacy, and a place to learn the real constraints. That learning feeds the products. Maestro, Forge, Mnemos, Agent Fabric, and Surfaces all make more sense when they are built against a real fleet instead of a whiteboard.
