Consensus is not overkill when the answer matters

By Bryce Murad · February 09, 2026

Why I ask more than one model

Single model confidence is cheap

A single model can sound certain while being wrong. Anyone who has used these systems for real work knows the feeling. The answer is clean. The tone is confident. The facts are sideways.

Consensus is my answer for higher stakes work. Ask multiple models. Compare the shape of the answers. Look for disagreement. Force the system to explain where the uncertainty lives.

Three answers are not automatically better

Running three models does not magically create truth. Three lazy answers are still lazy. The value comes from structured comparison: what did they agree on, what did they miss, and what would change the decision?

That is why Maestro treats consensus as a workflow, not a party trick. It is useful for architecture decisions, risk review, claims checking, and any task where a pretty answer can be dangerous.

The goal is less theater

I do not need every answer to be perfect. I need the system to make uncertainty visible. That is what good consensus does. It slows the moment down just enough to keep me from accepting a confident hallucination because it sounded convenient.
