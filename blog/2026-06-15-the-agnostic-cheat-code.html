The agnostic cheat code

June 15, 2026

The cheat code is your own local memory provider, and switching the model becomes free.

Once you have a memory that is yours, that runs on your hardware, that the agents read from and write to, the model becomes interchangeable. You can run a high-powered Claude Code session and the agent knows your context, your workspace, the repos that matter for the project, the data shape, the design history, the receipts. You can switch to a ChatGPT session, and the agent knows the same things, because the memory is the substrate, not the model. You can switch to an open-source agent on your own hardware, and the agent knows the same things, because the memory is the substrate, not the model.

This is the cheat code. The model is replaceable. The surface is replaceable. The dispatch engine is replaceable. The memory is not. The memory is the thing that survives every model change, every surface change, every dispatch change. The memory is the thing that is yours.

What the cheat code makes possible.

You can run a Claude Code session, switch mid-task to ChatGPT, and pick up where you left off. The new agent knows what the old agent knew. You can run an open-source Qwen on a DGX Spark, and the agent that runs on the local hardware has the same project context as the agent that runs on the frontier. You can hire a contractor, give them the memory key, and the contractor's agents read the same context your agents read. You can move from a Mac mini to a DGX Spark, and the substrate follows the hardware. The model changes. The surface changes. The dispatch changes. The substrate does not change. The substrate is the thing that does not change.

This speeds up intent-to-action in an immeasurable way. When the agent knows the context, the workspace, the data shape, the receipts, the previous attempts, the tradeoffs you have already considered, you do not have to re-explain any of it. You describe what you want. The agent builds it. You ship it. That is the loop. That is the speedup. The speedup is not the model. The speedup is the substrate.

Mnemos is the proof, in production, today.

Mnemos is the in-house long-term memory system that every agent I run reads from and writes to. It is the thing in production that has helped me the most. It is the reason the rest of the work is possible. The system has been in production across the fleet since the first quarter of the year. Every agent I run, every project I touch, every workflow I run, reads from and writes to Mnemos. The substrate is the substrate.

The numbers are not the point, but the numbers are the proof. Mnemos currently holds 107,907 memories across the fleet, served to 70 registered actors. Recall median latency over five mixed queries is 377 milliseconds. The system is the substrate. The substrate is in production. The substrate works.

What works in production is the proof. What is in production is the proof. What the agents reach for on every task is the proof. The agents reach for Mnemos on every task. The agents reach for the context before they reach for the model. The agents reach for the substrate before they reach for the surface. The substrate is the thing they reach for.

The four pillars are downstream of the cheat code.

Once the substrate is yours, the rest of the work is downstream. The model is interchangeable. The surface is interchangeable. The dispatch is interchangeable. The work is to make the substrate usable, to make the model choice free, to make the surface a place you want to type into, to make the dispatch a place you trust to handle the complex work unsupervised.

Maestro is the router that sits in front of the models. The choice of which model answers a prompt is a system design problem, not a vibes problem. Maestro knows what the local Qwen can do, knows what the frontier model can do, knows the cost of each, and routes accordingly. The dumb router is on purpose. The router is a config file with a few rules. That is what you want. Predictable, explainable, cheap. When you can switch the model for free, the router is the thing that picks the model. When the router picks the model, the substrate is the thing that survives the model choice. The substrate is the thing that does not need the model to be the substrate.

Surfaces is the thing that becomes your own Claude Code, without the subscription, as long as you have the hardware. The point of Surfaces is not the model. The point is the surface. The thing you type into. The thing that holds your context, your memory, your agents, your tools. The model is replaceable. The surface is yours. The surface reads from Mnemos. The surface writes to Mnemos. The surface is the thing that makes the substrate usable. The surface is the thing that turns the substrate into a tool.

Agent Fabric is the parallelized dispatch engine. Complex tasks deconstruct into intent, plan vigorously, execute parallelized, and test along the way. Always testing. Always receiving receipts. The work is not the agents. The work is the system that watches the agents. Agent Fabric is that system. It is the harness that makes agents trustworthy enough to run unsupervised. The harness reads from Mnemos. The harness writes to Mnemos. The harness is the thing that turns the substrate into a fleet. The substrate is the thing the fleet shares.

The four of them together, Mnemos, Maestro, Surfaces, Agent Fabric, are the local-first agent platform. Local-first is not a feature. Local-first is the position. The AI should run on your hardware, with your memory, pointed at your intentions. That is the line.

The three good endings and how the cheat code reaches them.

Max Tegmark mapped twelve ways the age of superintelligence can end. By my read, only three are actually good for people, and the line between them and the rest is whether the power is spread out and whether the AI is actually aligned to the person using it.

The first is the decentralized substrate. A world where the AI is everywhere, on everyone's hardware, pointed at everyone's intentions. No one can monopolize it, because it is not a thing that can be monopolized. The cheat code reaches the substrate. The cheat code is the substrate. The substrate is the position that scales to everyone. The substrate is the position that does not end in exploitation, because the exploitation requires concentration, and the cheat code is the opposite of concentration.

The second is the aligned guardian. An AI that looks out for us without running our lives. The cheat code does not reach the guardian. The guardian is a different problem. The guardian is the problem of governance, of accountability, of structural constraint. The guardian is the problem that the substrate does not solve. The guardian is the problem that the substrate is a precondition for, because the guardian is the thing that requires the substrate to be honest, and the substrate is the thing that is honest by construction.

The third is what Tegmark calls the enslaved god, except decentralized. A capability so vast that no one actor can monopolize it, that runs on a billion small pieces of hardware, that is the substrate underneath the world rather than the boss of it. This is the one I am most actively working toward, because it is the one that does not require the rest of the world to agree with me. The cheat code is the substrate. The substrate is the decentralized enslaved god. The decentralized enslaved god is the position I am betting on.

What I am not arguing.

I am not arguing that the substrate will save us without help. The substrate is necessary. The substrate is not sufficient. The substrate is a tool. The rest of the work is the work. The work is to build the substrate, to maintain the substrate, to make the substrate usable, to make the substrate trustworthy, to make the substrate cheap, to make the substrate resilient. The work is the work.

I am not arguing that the substrate replaces the work. The substrate is the position. The work is the position. The substrate is the thing that the work is built on. The work is the thing the substrate is built for. The work and the substrate are the same position. The work and the substrate are the same work.

The line.

The line is the only thing that matters. The line is whether the AI is yours. Is it on your hardware. Does it use your memory. Is it pointed at your intentions. The line is the test. The line is the only test.

The cheat code is your own local memory. The model is interchangeable. The surface is interchangeable. The dispatch is interchangeable. The substrate is not interchangeable. The substrate is yours. The substrate is the line.

I have a hand on the wheel. I am not a passenger in this. I am a participant. I build the substrate. I build the thing that is mine. I build the thing that is yours if you want it. I build the thing that is on your hardware, that uses your memory, that is pointed at your intentions. I build the line.

If you are building something that puts capable, aligned intelligence in more people's hands, I would love to hear from you. The conversation is the only way any of this gets better. Six months from now I will write the next post in this series. It will be the receipt. The receipt will be the work. The work will be the substrate. I will see you then.
