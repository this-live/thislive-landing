Why I Built This

By Bryce Murad · this.live

I want to close by explaining why I am doing any of this. Not the technical reasons. The human ones.

I got laid off in January. I had spent five years as a network architect building enterprise infrastructure for massive organizations. Financial services. Healthcare. Government. I was good at it. I knew my field. And one day, my position ended.

I had a choice. Send resumes. Or build something.

I chose to build. I set up an LLC. I started experimenting with autonomous agents. I watched an AI write code, debug errors, and deploy services. I realized that the gap between idea and execution was collapsing. One person with the right tools could build what used to require teams and funding.

So I built Maestro. Not because I wanted to start a SaaS company. Because I needed it to exist. I needed a way to run agents without going broke. Without compromising security. Without being a full-time DevOps engineer. And I knew other people needed it too.

I built Beacon because I saw a real problem. Ten million small businesses in the US without websites. A massive market of people who could benefit from a web presence but had no way to get one. AI makes that possible at a scale and price point that was unimaginable two years ago.

I built the SAQ bot because I coach a swim team and I was tired of answering the same questions forty times a season. Niche agents solve real problems for real communities.

I am not a visionary. I am a builder who got lucky with timing. The tools to do this exist right now. The models are good enough. The infrastructure is accessible. The only missing piece is the will to start.

If you are reading this and you have an idea, my advice is simple. Start building. Use the agents. Use the models. Let them help you. The barrier to entry for building software has never been lower.

That is what democratized AI means to me. Not chatbots. Not slightly better search. The ability for anyone with an idea to turn it into reality.

That is worth building for.

Thank you for reading. Now go build something.
