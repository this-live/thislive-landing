What I Am Tracking in AI Right Now

By Bryce Murad · this.live

The pace of AI right now is genuinely hard to keep up with, even for someone who lives inside it daily. I spend hours every week just staying current, and I still miss things. Here is what I am tracking and why it matters for anyone building with this technology.

Model context windows keep expanding. We are now at millions of tokens on some models. This changes what an agent can do in a single session. An entire codebase, an entire book, an entire conversation history. All of it fits in context at once. The implications for code review, research synthesis, and long-form analysis are massive. Tasks that required chunking and stitching now happen in one shot.

Reasoning models are getting better. Not just bigger, but actually better at step-by-step thinking. DeepSeek R1 proved that open-weight reasoning models can compete with closed ones. That democratizes access to high-quality reasoning. You no longer need OpenAI or Anthropic to get a model that thinks through problems carefully.

Tool use is becoming standard. Every major model now supports function calling, tool selection, and structured output. This means agents are not a hack anymore. They are a first-class use case that model providers are optimizing for. The tooling is getting better, faster, and more reliable every month.

Multimodal is maturing. Vision, audio, video. Agents can now look at screenshots, listen to audio, watch video clips. This opens up entirely new categories of tasks that were impossible a year ago. An agent that can see your UI and tell you what is wrong. An agent that can listen to a customer service call and summarize the issue. These are not demos anymore. They are products.

The infrastructure layer is catching up. Better orchestration frameworks, better memory systems, better routing, better caching. The gap between "cool demo" and "production system" is closing fast. What required a team of engineers six months ago can now be done by a solo builder with the right stack.

If you are not paying attention to this space, you are going to wake up in a world that has changed around you. The good news is the tools to participate are more accessible than ever. The bad news is the pace is only accelerating.

That is why I am building. Not to predict the future. To be ready for it. To have the infrastructure in place when the next breakthrough happens. Because it will happen. Probably next month. Possibly next week.

Stay current. Stay building. The wave is still rising.
