Why I built This.Live

By Bryce Murad · January 12, 2026

The first week after January

January was the line in the sand

I got laid off in January. That is the honest beginning of this chapter. I had spent five years as a network architect building enterprise infrastructure for large organizations across financial services, healthcare, and government. I was good at the work. I knew how to translate messy requirements into network designs, cost models, diagrams, RFP responses, and systems that could survive contact with real customers.

Then the job ended, and I had to decide whether I wanted to spend the next stretch trying to get back into the same lane or use the moment to build the thing I kept circling around anyway. This.Live started there. Not as a polished company story. More like a hard reset with a terminal open.

The first version was not the final shape

The original idea had live software energy around it, but by March the public site had turned into something more useful: a home for the AI-native software company I was actually building. The public launch happened in March. The real work started in January. That distinction matters because I do not want to pretend this has been running for years. It has not.

What I have been building since January is a set of products and infrastructure that lets a small operator do serious work with agents, local models, memory, routing, and disciplined verification. I am not trying to sell a fantasy about replacing people. I am trying to build leverage without losing judgment.

The human reason

The human reason is simple. I like building. I like coaching. I like taking something messy and turning it into a system that works. Swimming taught me that perfect practice is not romantic. It is repetitive, occasionally boring, and brutally honest. Network architecture taught me that production systems do not care how clever your diagram looked. They either route traffic or they do not.

This.Live is me applying those lessons to software, agents, and the next version of work. Hard work works. Truth matters. You help people when you can. You stay composed, and when it is time to compete, you compete.
