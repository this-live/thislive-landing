The problem it kills

Provider lock-in & runaway token cost

The rented stack optimizes the provider's margin, not yours. You take their model, their guardrails, their price — and you re-pay to process your own context every single call.

One vendor owns your intelligence

One API, one bill, one roadmap. When a provider neuters, deprecates, or reprices a model, your whole stack inherits the decision — and migrating means rebuilding around a new API.

Every call is an open-ended tax

Per-token pricing means persistent agents burn cash forever, even on work a local model could do for free. The bill scales with usage instead of falling as your stack matures.

Real capabilities

What Maestro actually does

One endpoint, many models

OpenAI-compatible /v1/chat/completions. Point any client at Maestro and routing happens implicitly — local models and frontier providers behind one URL.

Cost & latency aware

Routing weighs price and latency alongside quality. Cheap, fast, local paths handle what they can; the expensive path is the exception, not the default.

Consensus when it matters

Quorum scores and synthesizes across multiple models for the calls where a single answer is too risky to trust on its own.

BYOK, secrets stay yours

Bring your own keys to reach frontier models, or run fully local with none. The secret-scan redaction gate runs before any cloud egress.

Audit any decision

Every route is durable: a decision_id round-trips and the full receipt — model chosen, tier, cost — is fetchable after the fact.

A learning flywheel

A frozen corpus of ~63k historical routing labels grounds Vega's priors. The auto-labeling pipeline is wired onto live calls — its training signal is still maturing (see the ledger).

The routing path

One request in. The right model out — with a receipt.

A prompt enters the OpenAI-compatible endpoint, Vega scores it across seven tiers, and the call lands on the cheapest model that clears the bar — local first. Frontier egress passes the redaction gate; every route writes a durable decision.

  • Local-first. On-device models handle what they can; nothing leaves unless a frontier model truly earns the call.
  • Redaction before egress. The secret-scan gate is in the path, not an afterthought.
  • Durable by default. Each decision is fetchable at /v1/receipt/{id} with the model, tier, and cost.
