Cortex · the fleet & the engine

The fully-local fleet. The engine that runs on it.

Cortex is the nodes and the engine: a fully-local fleet of machines you own — a GPU node, a storage NAS, dev machines — running one provider-agnostic agentic stack. Control and privacy come from locality: your hardware, your models, your data. Six pillars run on this fleet under one canon — each stands alone, and together they form one local-first system you own.

The problem it kills

Your intelligence running on rented machines

When your models, context, and orchestration all live on infrastructure you don't own, control and privacy are someone else's promise — and a sprawl of parts with no shared canon quietly drifts into phantom maturity.

Your data and decisions live on their hardware

Control and privacy erode the moment your context, models, and routing run on machines you don't own. Locality is the only durable answer — a property of your own fleet, not a privacy policy you have to trust.

A fleet of parts that drift into phantom maturity

Run six independent components across several machines with no shared contracts and no maturity gates, and they quietly start claiming things they can't prove. Cortex is the canon that keeps the whole fleet honest.

The fleet & the canon

One canon at the center. Six pillars on your nodes.

The engine runs across a fleet you own; Cortex holds it together from the center. Shared contracts and maturity gates flow out to every pillar, and each pillar reports back against the same canon. They stand alone — and together they form one local-first system on your hardware.

  • Pillars run on your nodes. Models on a GPU node, memory on a NAS, agents on dev machines — local-first by construction.
  • Contracts flow out. TaskEnvelope, ExecutionReceipt, RouteDecision — defined once, imported everywhere.
  • Claims report back. Every pillar's public surface is gated against the same receipts.

Truth discipline

What's real today — and what's staged

The fleet runs and the canon governs it. Cortex sets the truth-discipline bar for the whole suite, so it holds itself to it first: every claim maps to a receipt, and the managed tier is labeled exactly as it is.
