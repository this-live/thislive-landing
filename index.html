You rent intelligence by the token. Persistent agents become an open-ended tax, and switching providers means rebuilding everything around their API.

The problem

A 200K window is not 200K of memory. Models degrade at the tail of long contexts, every session resets, and you pay to re-process your own data again and again.

Where I stand

Why this is built the way it is.

There's no sitting out AI. It's like economics. Whether you understand it or not, whether you choose to play or not, you're already in the game. The only real choice is whether you have a hand on the wheel.

Right now a handful of companies are deciding how the most capable technology in history gets aligned with the rest of us. I don't think they're evil. I think it's just how it's shaking out, and I think it's dangerous. As we move toward a world of real abundance, the threat was never killer robots. It's that a few people end up holding the keys to everything. Asymmetric advantage always ends in exploitation. That isn't pessimism, it's game theory.

Max Tegmark mapped twelve ways the age of superintelligence can end. By my read, only three are actually good for people: an egalitarian world where the gains are shared, a protector that looks out for us without running our lives, and what he calls the enslaved god, except decentralized. I'm aiming at any of the three, and ideally the first. I think you reach an egalitarian outcome the same way you avoid the bad ones: by putting a capable, aligned AI in everyone's hands instead of a few. Not one superintelligence owned by a company, but a system that every person owns and runs themselves, on their own hardware, pointed at their own intentions. When no one can monopolize it, no one can hoard what it produces. The line between the good endings and the rest is always the same. Is the power spread out, and is the AI actually aligned to the person using it.

Some serious researchers are fine with the endings where AI replaces us, whether it wipes us out or politely inherits the earth as our successors. They call it evolution. I don't. And I don't want the quiet endings either, the ones where we're kept safe, comfortable, and stripped of any reason to strive. A future where we lose ourselves is a loss whether it's loud or quiet. This.live wasn't founded to fight one bad outcome. It was founded to fight every one that isn't good.

So I'm building Cortex to be yours. Local, self-improving, running on your own hardware, aligned to your intentions from the start instead of patched in at the end. Take it, build whatever you want with it, and let it help you however you need.

No one person decides how this goes. But if enough of us each own a piece, build in the open, and trust that people are more self-healing than not, I think we get a real shot at one of the good endings. I'd rather have a hand in that than watch it happen to me.
