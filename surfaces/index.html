The problem it kills

Your AI is scattered across a dozen apps

A chat app here, a voice assistant there, a terminal somewhere else — each with its own login, its own memory, its own bill. None of them know what the others did, and none of them are really yours.

A surface for every task, none connected

One app to chat, another to dictate, another to run jobs — each a silo with its own state. You're the integration layer, copy-pasting context between tools that should share it.

Chatboxes, not a life OS

Most AI surfaces are a blank prompt waiting for you to do the work. They don't organize your day, your tasks, or your fleet — they just answer, then forget.

How it works

Three surfaces, one backend, one app

Each surface is purpose-built — voice, life, fleet — but they all speak to a single shared backend and converge into one installable desktop shell with six tabs.

01

Pocket Agent

A live voice daemon turns spoken intent into action, with pluggable voice backends — running on the shared :19000 backend.

02

Personal Life OS

Life-first tasks, not a blank chatbox. PLO organizes the day around what you're actually doing, and ships to Android via Capacitor.

03

Fleet Terminal

Speech-to-text plus a local dispatch multiplexer — talk to your whole fleet from one terminal, running live on its own port.

04

One desktop app

An installable Electron shell with six tabs, including a live Voice console — the three surfaces converged into one window you own.
