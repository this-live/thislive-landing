Philosophy

What I think, and why I build the way I build.

A reference for the things I argue and the position I am building from. Long form. Read with a coffee. The line is at the end.

The AI economy exists whether you like it or not, and it is the greatest force multiplier of our time. There is no sitting it out, the way there is no sitting out participation in the economy writ large. Whether you understand it or not, you are already in the game. The only real choice is whether you have a hand on the wheel.

If you read this and you think the answer is to wait it out, the rest of this page is not for you. If you read this and you think the answer is to participate, intentionally, with the work in your own hands, then we have a lot to talk about.

The argument

The danger was never killer robots. The danger is that a few people end up holding the keys to everything. Asymmetric advantage always ends in exploitation. That is not pessimism. It is game theory. The actors with the most leverage do not have a moral obligation to use it for the rest of us. They have an economic incentive to use it for themselves. The system, left alone, produces the concentrated outcome. To get the egalitarian outcome, the system has to be designed differently. The system has to be designed so the leverage is in the hands of the many, not the few.

Max Tegmark mapped twelve ways the age of superintelligence can end. By my read, only three are actually good for people, and the line between them and the rest is whether the power is spread out and whether the AI is actually aligned to the person using it.

The first is the decentralized substrate. A world where the AI is everywhere, on everyone's hardware, pointed at everyone's intentions. No one can monopolize it, because it is not a thing that can be monopolized. The substrate is the system underneath, the way TCP/IP is the system underneath the internet. You do not own TCP/IP. You build on it. The substrate is the abundance outcome. The substrate is the one that does not end in exploitation, because the exploitation requires concentration, and the substrate is the opposite of concentration.

The second is the aligned guardian. An AI that looks out for us without running our lives. An AI that is bound to our flourishing and not to the profit of its owners. An AI that watches over the species without becoming its keeper. This is the one the serious alignment researchers are most excited about, and it is a real possibility, but it requires a particular kind of governance, a particular kind of accountability, and a particular kind of structural constraint that the current commercial AI market is not incentivized to build.

The third is what Tegmark calls the enslaved god, except decentralized. A capability so vast that no one actor can monopolize it, that runs on a billion small pieces of hardware, that is the substrate underneath the world rather than the boss of it. This is the one I am most actively working toward, because it is the one that does not require the rest of the world to agree with me. The substrate can be built by anyone, including me, in a corner, with a small team, on real hardware, in production. The guardian requires consensus. The substrate requires only a contributor. I am a contributor.

I am not interested in the quiet endings either, the ones where we are kept safe, comfortable, and stripped of any reason to strive. A future where we lose ourselves is a loss whether it is loud or quiet. Some serious researchers are fine with the endings where AI replaces us, whether it wipes us out or politely inherits the earth as our successors. They call it evolution. I do not. Evolution is not a moral argument. I want a hand in the ending. I would rather have a hand in it than watch it happen to me.

What I am not arguing

I am not arguing that AI is bad. AI is the most useful tool I have ever used, and I have been using it for years. AI is not the threat. The threat is the concentration of the substrate in the hands of a few. The threat is the asymmetric advantage that concentration produces. The threat is the capture of the substrate by the actors who have the most to gain from the substrate not being distributed. AI is a tool. The tool can be used well or used poorly. The tool is more useful in more hands. The tool is the answer, not the threat. The threat is the hoarding.

I am not arguing that the substrate will save us without help. The substrate is necessary. The substrate is not sufficient. The substrate is a tool. The rest of the work is the work.

The other things I think

I am pragmatic. The other threats are not the AI threat, but they are real, and they are not going to wait for us to solve the AI one first. Food supply. Forever chemicals. Arid land. Microplastics. Drug safety. Wars. Cultural misunderstanding. Geopolitics. I believe humanity is racing toward extinction in many ways, but also that we have the means and the increasing knowledge to fix these things. We do not have the alignment. We do not have the time. We do not have the excuse.

I am not an accelerationist. I am not a decelerationist. I am a builder with a posture. The posture is the substrate. The substrate is the position that puts a hand on the wheel. The posture is the position that does not wait for the rest of the world to agree. The posture is the position that builds the substrate, locally, now, with the resources I have, pointed at the intentions I have, and lets the rest of the world do the same with theirs.

The people I learn from

I learn from the people who did the work. The athletes taught me the practice. The philosophers taught me the frame. The founders taught me the operator discipline. The modern operators taught me how to think about the current era.

The athletes

Michael Jordan is the standard for the competitive posture. The way he used every loss as fuel, the way he treated practice as the craft, is the standard I hold myself to. I do not have Jordan's talent. I have Jordan's posture. The posture is the part that transfers.

Stephen Curry is the standard for the underdog bet. The scouting reports said he was too small, too thin, not athletic enough. He bet on himself. He did the work. He changed the geometry of the game. The lesson is not that he changed the game. The lesson is that he bet on himself before the world bet on him.

Muhammad Ali is the standard for the performance under pressure. Ali's trash talk was not bravado. Ali's trash talk was a tool. The lesson is not the talk. The lesson is the use of the talk. The work you do in public is part of the work.

Demetrious Johnson is the standard for the patient technical excellence. DJ is the most technically complete fighter in MMA history. DJ is not the most powerful. DJ is the most patient. The lesson is that the technical excellence compounds, and the patience compounds, and the compounding compounds.

Tiger Woods is the standard for the discipline that survives the injury. Tiger rebuilt himself from injury after injury, process after process. The lesson is not the comeback. The lesson is that the discipline is the thing that survives.

The philosophers

Ralph Waldo Emerson is the standard for the self-reliance. The work is the work, and the work is yours, and the work is not the consensus.

John Stuart Mill is the standard for the harm principle. The AI should be limited to preventing harm to the user, and to the others affected by the user's actions. The AI should not be limited to enforcing the preferences of a third party. The AI is yours.

Søren Kierkegaard is the standard for the leap. The reason is not sufficient. The position is the same position I take when I argue that the substrate requires the commitment, not just the argument. The substrate is the build. The build is the leap.

Friedrich Nietzsche is the standard for the critique of the comfortable morality. The lesson is not the will to power. The lesson is the critique. The lesson is that the morality that says "be comfortable" is the morality that says "do not build."

Immanuel Kant is the standard for the categorical imperative. The question is not "what do I want to do." The question is "what should be done, if everyone did it." The substrate is the position that should be done if everyone did it.

John Locke is the standard for the natural rights. The rights are not granted by the state. The rights are the substrate. The AI is the same argument. The capability is not granted by the operator. The capability is the substrate.

Ray Dalio is the standard for the radical transparency. The truth is more useful than the comfort. The substrate is the position that requires the truth to be on the table.

Richard Werner is the standard for the critique of the money. The money is the credit. The money is the policy. The substrate is the same argument. The substrate is not what you think the substrate is. The substrate is the position.

John Mearsheimer is the standard for the great power politics. The great powers do what the great powers can do. The substrate is the position that says the answer to the capability is the counter-capability. The substrate is the counter-capability that is in the hands of the many.

The founders

Steve Jobs is the standard for the craft. The work is the work, and the work is the craft, and the craft is the thing you do not compromise on. The substrate is the thing you do not compromise on. The substrate is the thing you ship, and the substrate is the thing you ship well.

Jesse Itzler is the standard for the discipline that compounds. The discipline is the work, and the work is the work. The lesson is that the commitment is the thing that compounds. The commitment is the thing that is the substrate.

Elon Musk is the standard for the first-principles. You can reason from the substrate up. The substrate is the first principles. The substrate is the thing you reason from. The substrate is the thing you do not take on faith.

The modern operators

David Friedberg is the standard for the production layer. The production layer is the food. The production layer is the energy. The production layer is the atoms. The bits ride on top of the atoms. The substrate is the production layer. The substrate is the thing you do not skip.

Lex Fridman is the standard for the long-form truth-seeking. The format is the long form. The format is the patience. The substrate is the same format. The substrate is the willingness to sit with the question for the time it takes to answer the question.

Chamath Palihapitiya is the standard for the asymmetric bet. The bet is the bet if the upside is large and the downside is small. The substrate is the asymmetric bet. The substrate is the position that the upside is the substrate, and the downside is the abstraction.

The line

The line is the only thing that matters. The line is whether the AI is yours. Is it on your hardware. Does it use your memory. Is it pointed at your intentions. Is it yours, in the same way your house is yours, in the same way your tools are yours, in the same way your time is yours.

If a piece of writing keeps the line, the writing is right. If it loses the line, the writing is wrong. The line is the test. The line is the only test.

I have a hand on the wheel. I am not a passenger in this. I am a participant. I build the substrate. I build the thing that is mine. I build the thing that is yours if you want it. I build the thing that is on your hardware, that uses your memory, that is pointed at your intentions. I build the line.

I would rather have a hand in it than watch it happen to me. If you are building something that puts capable, aligned intelligence in more people's hands, I would love to hear from you. If you are building something that puts it in fewer, I would love to hear from you anyway, because the conversation is the only way any of this gets better.
