Forge · the model factory

Your own models, trained on your own work.

Forge is the factory that turns your fleet's own labels into local specialists — so you stop depending on a provider's model. The factory is built end-to-end and gated so it cannot lie: nothing is promoted without passing an eval. The base model already serves on your own GPU; the first adapters are in training.

The problem it kills

Model dependency — and agents that ship slop

Rent your intelligence and you inherit a provider's roadmap: their model, their reprices, their deprecations. And a model you can't specialize keeps making the same mistakes on your domain, confidently.

You can't improve a model you don't own

When the only path is a provider's API, your stack can never get better at your work. Every quirk of your domain is re-learned in-context, every call, forever — and you pay for it each time.

Agents that confidently ship slop

An unspecialized model has no bar to clear and no memory of getting it wrong. Forge is built with no-bypass eval and safety gates, so a candidate can't be promoted unless it measurably beats the bar. The gate is what keeps it honest.

How it works

Curate, train, eval-gate, serve

A factory with eight lanes and a cloud harness, gated so nothing ships unproven. The base model serves today; the adapter steps light up as the first specialists clear their evals.

01

Curate from your work

Your fleet's own labels become training examples. Curate gates plus depth and safety gates filter what's allowed in — built across eight lanes.

02

Train in the cloud

A Modal cloud training harness runs the pipeline — proven on a CPU end-to-end run at $0. The pipeline runs; the first adapters are in training.

03

Eval gate

Promotion is eval-gated: a candidate must measurably beat the base before it can be served. Registry adapters stay planned until they pass.

04

Serve locally

The base model serves on your own GPU today. Routing local-first to a Forge adapter lights up the moment an adapter is trained and clears its gate.

Factory built end-to-end

Eight lanes, the Modal harness, curate gates, depth and safety gates, and an alignment gate — all built. The pipeline exists and runs.

No-bypass eval gates

A candidate adapter cannot be promoted unless it measurably beats the base on an eval. The gate is structural — there's no path around it.

Base served on your GPU

Qwen3.6 serves on a local GPU node via SGLang at 262k context, base-only. The base code-review eval baseline is measured live at 0.4727.

The cost-down flywheel

Maestro labels feed harvest, curate, train, eval-gate, serve, and route — documented with live vs pending marked per link. The more you use it, the cheaper it's designed to get.

Autonomous harvest loop

A harvest loop is deployed and fired autonomously from a Sparky cron (a non-Hermes scheduler) — the intake side of the flywheel runs on its own.

Verticalized specialists

The factory is built to produce local adapters specialized to your domains — so the next generation of your stack is owned and tuned to your work, not rented.
