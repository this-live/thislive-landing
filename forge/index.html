How it works

A loop you own: curate, train, eval-gate, serve

Eight lanes and a cloud harness wired into one closed loop, gated so nothing ships unproven. The base model serves today; the adapter steps light up as the first specialists clear their evals — and the routed work feeds the next pass.

01

Curate from your work

Your fleet's own routed work becomes training fuel. Curate, depth, and safety gates filter what's allowed in — the first stage of a loop you own, built across eight lanes.

02

Train in the cloud

A Modal cloud training harness turns curated fuel into a candidate adapter — proven on a CPU end-to-end run at $0. The pipeline runs; the first adapters are in training.

03

Eval gate

Promotion is eval-gated: a candidate must measurably beat the base before it can be served. Registry adapters stay planned until they pass — the gate is what keeps the loop honest.

04

Serve locally, route to first

The base serves on your own GPU today. A gate-passing adapter is served locally and routed to first — and the routed work becomes the next adapter's training data. The loop closes.
